lung cancer begins when cells in the lung mutate (change), causing them to grow uncontrollably and form a tumor. lung cancer typically spreads (metastasizes) to other parts of the body before it is detected, which is why it is often difficult to treat.

Smoking is the most common cause of lung cancer, but nonsmokers can also get the disease. other risk factors include exposure to secondhand smoke, radon gas, asbestos, and air pollution.

There are two main types of lung cancer: non-small cell lung cancer (nsclc) and small cell lung cancer (sclc). nsclc is the most common type, accounting for about 85 percent of all cases. sclc is less common but more aggressive, and it tends to spread more quickly than nsclc.

Lung cancer symptoms can include:

* a cough that does not go away or gets worse over time
* coughing up blood or rust-colored sputum (phlegm)
* shortness of breath
* chest pain or pressure
* hoarseness
* recurrent respiratory infections such as bronchitis or pneumonia
* wheezing
* weight loss without trying
* fatigue
