    # Overview of Hemostasis

    # primary hemostasis 初級止血

    # secondary hemostasis 二級止血

    # primary hemostasis 初級止血

    • 血小板的正常功能 : 👮監測血管內皮的完整性並在血管損傷部位形成栓塞以阻止和防止血液滲漏到血管外
    • 沒有血管損傷 : 前列環素（prostaglandin）和內皮細胞分泌的一氧化氮的抑制🔐
    • 血管損傷和內皮損傷
      • 交感神經系統被啟動並引起血管收縮
      • collagen, fibronectin, and laminin : exposed to circulating blood
      • 👷血小板附在暴露的內皮下基質蛋白上，開始止血栓形成過程

    # secretion of storage granule content

    • 血小板顆粒 : 含有數千種蛋白質，啟動後釋放
    • 3 major secretory granules
      • alpha-granules
        • 凝血因子（I、II、V 和 XI）、
        • fibrinolysis system components
        • coagulation inhibitors
        • complement components (C1 inhibitor, C3, and C4)
      • delta-granules
        • ADP
      • lysosomes

    # during platelet activation 血小板活化

    • （ADP、thromboxane A2）啟動信號通路，將 GPIIb/IIIa 轉化為 divalent fibrinogen
    • fibrinogen 👉 聚集/凝聚

    # platelet aggregation 血小板聚集

    • GPIIb/IIIa
      • 募集額外的血小板
    • transmembrane ADP receptors P2Y1 and P2Y12
      • 血小板形態學變化
      • 血小板聚集

    # secondary hemostasis 二級止血

    • 沒流血時：
      • inactive precursor enzymes (zymogens)
      • 在血漿中循環
    • 流血時：
      • 導致fibrin polymerization和血小板活化，
      • 形成clot
    • 2 個途徑 :
      • extrinsic
      • intrinsic

    # Coagulation Factor - I

    1. I - fibrinogen
    2. II - prothrombin
    1. III - tissue thromboplastin (tissue factor)
    1. IV - ionized calcium ( Ca++ )
    2. V - labile factor or proaccelerin
    • PL : platelet membrane phospholipid
    1. VII - stable factor or proconvertin

    # Coagulation Factor - II

    1. VIII - antihemophilic factor
    2. IX - plasma thromboplastin component, Christmas factor
    3. X - Stuart-Prower factor
    4. XI - plasma thromboplastin antecedent
    5. XII - Hageman factor
    1. XIII - fibrin-stabilizing factor

    # Extrinsic pathway

    • 血管損傷或單核細胞啟動導致 VII（VIIa）啟動
    • 啟動 IX (intrinsic）和 X（common）

    # Intrinsic pathway

    • 由 XII 轉化為 XIIa 開始
    • XI 和 IX 逐步轉化為其活性形式
    • VIIIa （由thrombin啟動）
      • 在活化的血小板表面作為 IXa 的Cofactor
      • 促進 X to Xa

    # 然後兩條路徑匯聚到Common Pathwya共同路徑

    • X啟動為Xa
    • Xa 將prothrombin轉化為thrombin，cofactor Va 可加速反應
    • thrombin將fibrinogen轉化為fibrin
    • fibrin clot 形成

    # tertiary hemostasis or clot stabilization 三級止血或凝塊穩定

    • thrombin的將 XIII 啟動為 XIIIa
    • XIIIa cross-links stabilizing fibrin

    # Fibrinolysis 纖維蛋白溶解（凝塊溶解）

    • tissue-type plasminogen (tPA) 或 uPA :
      • plasminogen to plasmin
    • plasmin切割fibrin釋放fibrin degradation products(FDP) and D-dimer
    • fibrinolysis速率取決於 tPA 濃度，該濃度由以下因素控制:
      • 內皮分泌 tPA 的速率
      • 肝臟清除 tPA 的速率
      • tPA inhibitor、PAI-1的濃度
    • 破壞 tPA 的分泌、抑制和清除之間的平衡可導致 :
      • 出血（fibrinolysis亢進）
      • 血栓形成（fibrinolysis減退）

    # Prothrombin Time (PT) and INR

    • VII（外源性途徑）、因子 II、V 和 X，以及fibrinogen（共同途徑）
    • PT 的參考範圍因試劑和儀器而異；通常在 10 秒到 13 秒之間
    • INR = (patient’s PT/ Control PT)ISI

    # causes of PT prolongation

    1. underlying conditions
    2. anticoagulants
    3. acute dilutional coagulopathy
    # Shortened PT may suggest a hypercoagulable state, but it is not used to guide patient care in practice.

    # 1. PT prolongtaion : underlying conditions

    • factor VII (extrinsic pathway)
    • factors V, VII, and/or X, prothrombin, and/or fibrinogen (common pathway)
    • vitamin K deficiency
    • liver disease
    • afibrinogenemia, hypofibrinogenemia, or dysfibrinogenemia disseminated intravascular coagulation
    • elevated fibrin degradation products
    • some lupus anticoagulant (uncommon)/antiphospholipid antibodies

    # 2. PT prolongtaion : anticoagulants

    • vitamin K antagonists including warfarin
    • factor Xa inhibitors including rivaroxaban and to a lesser extent, apixaban
    • unfractionated heparin (generally at high doses; PT/INR not typically affected by low-molecular-weight heparin)
    • parenteral or oral direct thrombin inhibitors such as bivalirudin or dabigatran

    # limitations of PT

    • inability to detect most fibrinogen disorders
    • there are variations among PT assay reagents with different sensitivities to factor deficiencies, thus
      • normal PT does not rule out factor deficiencies
      • prolonged PT does not necessarily reflect bleeding risk

    # Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT)

    • factors XII, XI, IX, VIII (intrinsic pathway)
    • factors II, V, and X, and fibrinogen (common pathway)
    • screening : bleeding disorders due to deficiencies of, or inhibitors to coagulation factors
    • monitoring : unfractionated heparin therapy
    • assessment : disseminated intravascular coagulation
    • monitoring : liver disease status
    • screening : lupus anticoagulant

    # causes of aPTT prolongation

    • factor XII deficiency : most common
    • acquired or inherited deficiencies or inhibition
      • factors XI, IX (hemophilia B),
      • factors VIII (including hemophilia A), X, II, and fibrinogen (though aPTT does not detect most fibrinogen disorders)
    • vitamin K deficiency
    • presence of lupus anticoagulant
    • severe von Willebrand disease
    • disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
    • liver disease

    # anticoagulants cause aPTT prolognation

    • high doses of vitamin K antagonists, although prothrombin time is more sensitive for vitamin K antagonist
    • heparin (particularly unfractionated heparin)
    • bivalirudin or dabigatran
    • factor Xa inhibitors such as apixaban and rivaroxaban

    # Thrombin Time (TT)

    • detection of hypofibrinogenemia and dysfibrinogenemia
    • detection of thrombin inhibitors
    • other testing usually provide sufficient information without the use of TT in patients requiring investigation of inherited disorders of secondary hemostasis

    # Fibrinogen Assay

    normal fibrinogen concentration ranges from 200 mg/dL (2 g/L) to 400 mg/dL (4 g/L)

    • afibrinogenemia, hypofibrinogenemia, and dysfibrinogenemia
    • liver disease
    • disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
    • primary fibrinolysis

    # fibrinogen <100 mg/dL : Cryoprecipitate

    • 1 unit of cryo per 5kg patient weight will increase fibrinogen by about 100 mg/dL

    # D-dimer Test

    • exclusion of venous thromboembolism (VTE) in patients with low clinical probability by clinical prediction scores
    • prediction of risk of recurrent VTE, thus aiding in decision making regarding duration of anticoagulation therapy in patients with VTE
    • diagnosis and management of disseminated intravascular coagulation
    • exclusion of acute aortic dissection
    • monitoring for thrombotic complications in patients with severe infections and sepsis

    Freund Y, Cohen-Aubart F, Bloom B. Acute Pulmonary Embolism: A Review. JAMA. 2022;328(13):1336-1345. doi:10.1001/jama.2022.16815

    # cutoff (typically < 500 mcg/L) to define negative D-dimer assay

    • higher threshold (< 1,000 mcg/L) in patients with low clinical pretest probability
    • cutoff of < 500 mcg/L may be used in patients with moderate pretest probability

    # Other conditions of elevtaed D-Dimer

    • being an inpatient
    • advanced age
    • cancer
    • disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)
    • infection
    • inflammatory conditions
    • recent surgery or trauma
    • liver disease, renal disease
    • atrial fibrillation
    • stroke
    • thrombolytic therapy

    # Mixing study:

    • useful if ⬆️ PT or PTT
    • mix Pt’s plasma 1:1 with normal plasma
    • and retest
      • PT/PTT normalizes
        • 👉 factor deficiency
      • PT/PTT remains elevated
        • 👉 factor inhibitor

    # Coagulation factor levels:

    • useful if mixing study suggests factor deficiency
    • DIC → all factors consumed ∴ ↓ factors V and VIII
    • Liver disease → ↓ all factors except VIII ∴ ↓ factor V, normal factor VIII
    • Vitamin K deficiency → ↓ factors II, VII, IX, X (and protein C, S); ∴ normal V and VIII

    VIII: by vascular, glomerular, and tubular endothelium, and the sinusoidal cells of the liver

    # DIC screen:

    • ↓ fibrinogen (consumed)
    • fibrin degradation products (FDPs, ⊕ from intense fibrinolysis),
    • ↑ D-dimer (more specific FDP test that detects degradation of X-linked fibrin)
