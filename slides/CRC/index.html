    # Epidemiology

    • ~135000 estimated annual new cases of CRC in US in 2016: 95000 colon, 39000 rectal
    • ~49,000 annual deaths, accounting for 8.3% of all CA death Highest incidence in Australia & New Zealand, Europe & North America

    # Risk Factors

    • Age, increasing incidence w/ age w/ relative rise starting at age 40â€“50
    • Familial syn (FAP, Lynch, MUTYH-associated polyposis, Peutzâ€“Jeghers, Juvenile polyposis) (see Lynch/FAP chapter)
    • Personal or FHx of CRC or adenomatous polyps
    • IBD: UC > CD, increasing risk 8â€“10 y after initial dx

    # Other associations

    • high red meat intake,
    • diabetes,
    • long-term immunosuppression,
    • EtOH, tobacco;
    • although classically thought to be a risk factor, prospective studies have not clearly demonstrated an association between low dietary fiber intake & colon CA (JAMA 2005, 294:2849)

    # Prevention

    # ASA & NSAIDs

    • Potential protective effect of ASA & NSAIDs on development of colonic adenomas & CRC

    # Diet

    • Potential protective benefit of avoidance of red meat, limited caloric intake

    # Pathology

    # Adenoma-CA sequence

    Most common pathway, adenomas on avg take 10 y to develop (Cell 1990;61:759). Accumulation of genetic mts (eg, APC: TP53) leads to tumorigenesis; risk of CRC â†‘ w/ adenoma size, #, & histology (villous > tubular)

    # Serrated polyp pathway (CpG Island Methylation Phenotype or CIMP)

    Alternative route for CRC development through serrated adenomas

    # Molecular Classification

    • Chromosomal instability (hereditary & sporadic): Gain of function mts in oncogenes (eg, KRAS, & loss of function mts in tumor suppressor genes (eg, APC, TP53, DCC, SMAD4, SMAD2), no MSI

    # Clinical Presentation

    • Sx are generally nonspecific & many pts are asx until advanced disease
    • Distal colon: hematochezia, abdominal pain, obstruction, overflow diarrhea
    • Proximal colon: Fe deficiency anemia, melena, hematochezia, obstruction (rare)
    • Met disease: RUQ pain, ascites, wt loss, fatigue
    • Other sx: Sx from local invasion (bladder, small bowel); may mimic diverticulitis; FUO; S. bovis bacteremia & C. septicum sepsis

    # Preferred by most guidelines

    Colonoscopy every 5â€“10 y starting at 50, highest Se & Sp, â†“ mortality in RCT (NEJM 2012;366:687); screening past 85 not typically recommended or if the individual has serious medical comorbidities

    # Other options

    CT colonography every 5 y; flexible sigmoidoscopy every 5 y; FOBT annually; fecal immunochemical test (FIT) annually; stool DNA (optimal interval uncertain)

    # Screening in Increased Risk Populations (See Lynch/FAP chapter) Diagnostic Evaluation

    • Colonoscopy
    • CT of chest abdomen & pelvis
    • Hepatic function panel, CEA
    • KRAS/NRAS/BRAF mutant status only in pts w/ met dz
    • MSI testing or IHC for MMR proteins for all pts

    # Staging and Prognosis

    • Right-sided tumors generally have a worse prognosis
