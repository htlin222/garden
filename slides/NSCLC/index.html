    # Review of Non-small cell lung cancer

    R3林協霆

    # Epidemiology and Risk Factors

    • 22萬人/年 cases w/ 16萬 US deaths,
    • #1 cause of cancer mortality
    • 70% stage III/IV
    • Cigarette smoking:
      • 85% of all cases
      • ↑ w/ intensity & duration of smoking,
    • previous RT,
    • 多環芳香烴, 氡(地下室), metals, synergy w/ smoking + asbestos

    # Pathology

    # Adenocarcinomas of the lung (ADCL):

    60% of all lung cancer, 最常見 ↑ incidence, a/w 肥大性關節病變 hypertrophic osteoarthropathy, IHC: +TTF1 & +Napsin A, −p40

    # Squamous cell lung cancers (SQCLC):

    20% of all lung strongly a/w smoking, a/w hypercalcemia 2° to PTHrP release, IHC: –TTF1, +p40, +p63

    # Others:

    • Adenosquamous (<4%),
    • Sarcomatoid (<1%),
    • Large Cell Neuroendocrine Carcinoma (<6%),
    • Carcinoids (<5%)
      • uncommon neuroendocrine epithelial malignancies

    # Molecular Biology

    # KRAS Mt:

    • 25% of ADCL,
    • a/w smoking (transversions: G12C, G12V) but present in 6% of never-smokers (transitions: G12D).
    • Confers EGFR TKI resistance (CCR 2008;14:5731) -> 不良預後
    • No overlap w/ EGFR Mts, ALK/ROS1 rearrangements.

    # EGFR Mt:

    21% all pts, 43% never-smokers w/ ADCL,

    • exon 19 deletions LREA (45%)
    • exon 21 L858R (40%),
    • exon 18 (G719X) : 二代
    • exon 21 (L861Q), confer EGFR TKI Sn,
      • initially described in female Asian never-smokers but also present in smokers. 👩‍🦱
    • Exon 20 insertions: 預後不良😥
    • Acquired T790M Mt confers EGFR TKI resistance after 8–16 mos

    # ALK Rearrangements:

    • 2–7%
    • a/w younger males 👦
    • never/light smoking🚬 hx,
    • w/ signet-ring ADCL histology, resistant to EGFR TKI,
    • confers Sn to crizotinib (NEJM 2010;363:1693).
    • Not usually found in SCC but can be in mixed histology.

    # ROS 1 Rearrangements:

    • 1–2% of ADCL, younger women 👱‍♀️, never smokers🚭.
    • Usually negative for EGFR, KRAS, & ALK rearrangements (JCO 2012;30:863)

    # Other actionable aberrations:

    • BRAF 2% : Dabrafenib + trametinib (Lancet Onc 2016;17:984),
    • RET Fusions 1% : Selpercatinib,
    • HER2 Amp/Mt 3%, PIK3CA <1%,
    • MET Amp <1%, MET exon 14 Mts 3–4%

    # SQCLCs:

    • FGFR1(Fibroblast growth factor receptor 1) Amp 20%,
    • DDR2 Mt 4%, potential Sn to dasatinib (CML)
    • SOX2 Amp 8%
    • PIK3CA Mt 4%
    • PTEN Mt 10%
    • AKT Mt 6%

    (Nature 2012;489:519; JCO 2012;30:abstr 7505)

    # Screening and Prevention

    • 每年 low-dose CT: 20% mortality benefit in National Lung Screening Trial, NNT = 320 (篩320個人救一個人)
      • Ages 55–74 (up to 80 if good health),
      • ≥30 pack y, quit <15 y ago
    • Smoking cessation: Most effective method of prevention (including 2nd lung primary)

    # Clinical Manifestations

    • 10% 無症狀、意外發現by imaging 📸
    • Cough, hemoptysis, dyspnea, postobstructive pneumonia
    • Regional spread: pleural effusion, Pancoast syndrome (apical tumors), SVC syndrome
    • Extrathoracic metastases: 🧠brain, 🦴bone, liver, adrenal
    • Paraneoplastic syndromes: (1)SIADH, (2)hypercalcemia, (2)hypertrophic pulmonary osteoarthropathy

    # Diagnostic and Staging Evaluation

    # Imaging:

    • CT chest down to adrenals, PET scan to evaluate for distant metastasis, MR brain for pts treated w/ curative intent or symptoms,
    • PFTs preoperatively
    • Tissue:
      • bronchoscopy,
      • CT-guided needle bx, thoracentesis, or VATS.
    • check PD-L1
    • comprehensive molecular testing; NGS preferred.
    • Proceeding to surgery w/o bx is appropriate for Stage I.

    # Mediastinal evaluation:

    Considered for nodes >1 cm on CT or PET+ via EBUS-guided bronchoscopy or mediastinoscopy

    # Molecular Testing:

    • KRAS
    • EGFR
    • ALK
    • ROS1
    • BRAF
    • MET, RET, BRAF, HER2

    # Staging

    • Stage I : T1-2N0.
    • Stage IIA : T2N0
    • Stage IIB : T1-2N1, T3N0.
    • Stage IIIA: T1-2N2, T3N1, T4N0-1.
    • Stage IIIB: T1-2N3, T3-4N2.
    • Stage IIIC: T3-4N3.
    • Stage IV: Contralateral lung nodule (M1a), pleural or pericardial metastasis/effusion (M1a), single extrathoracic met (M1b), multiple extrathoracic mets (M1c)

    # Management:

    # Pathologic Stage I to IIIA (N1)

    # Surgery:

    Lobectomy w/ mediastinal node dissection preferred. If + margins, re-resection preferred over RT.

    # RT:

    If medically inoperable:

    • SBRT(身體立體定位放射治療), standard RT (60 Gy = 6000 cGy) radiofrequency or cryoablation.

    test

    # Adjuvant Chemo:

    • Not recommended for Stage IA;
    • consider for Stage IB (med onc consult mandatory). ↑ OS for Stage II–IIIA, CISplatin doublet for 4 cycles (LACE JCO 2008;26:3552),
    • most studies used CIS/vinorelbine (NEJM 2005;352:2589);
    • CIS/pemetrexed & CIS/docetaxel w/ similar efficacy & ↑ tolerability.

    # Adjuvant Targeted Therapy:

    • EGFR TKIs have DFS benefit, OS benefit inconclusive

    # port-op RT:

    • N2 nodes, +margins. Radiation Oncology consultation mandatory for N2.

    # Multiple Lung Cancers:

    • Need to bx all lesions.

    # Management:

    # Pathologic Stage IIIA (N2) to IIIB Disease

    Sequence of surgery, RT & chemo varies.

    # Induction:

    If resectable, neoadjuvant chemo to best response followed by resection + PORT. Neoadjuvant chemoRT does not improve outcomes.

    # Concurrent Chemoradiation:

    If unresectable (multiple +N2 LN>3 cm), definitive concurrent chemoradiation (JCO 1999;17:2692),

    # regimens:

    CIS/pemetrexed, CIS/vinblastine, CIS/etoposide, carboplatin/paclitaxel.

    # Pancoast tumor:

    ChemoRT (CIS/etoposide + 45 Gy) followed by surgery (T3N0-1) improves resectability (SWOG 9416 JCO 2007;25:313). If unresectable (T4N0-1), chemoRT.

    # Management:

    # Stage IV Metastatic Disease

    # Median OS:

    • 6-mo BSC (best supportive care)
    • 10-mo plat doublet,
    • 12-mo plat doublet + bevacizumab or necitumumab (NEJM 2006;355:2542; Lancet Onc 2015;16:763)

    # Initial Tx:

    -Pembrolizumab if PD-L1>50% (response rate~45%) (NEJM 2016;375:1823).

    • If PD-La <50%, 4–6 cycles of platinum doublet (response rate~30%).

    # Adenocarcinomas:

    • CIS/peme ± bevacizumab (if eligible: no-SQCLC, no hemoptysis)

    # SQCLC:

    • CIS/gem ± necitumumab (SQUIRE Lancet Onc 2015;16:763)
    • Carboplatin/paclitaxel (any histology)

    # Maintenance Tx:

    • For complete remission, partial remission, strable disease after 4–6 cycles of platinum doublet.
    • Discontinue platinum.
    • Continue peme ↑ OS (PARAMOUNT JCO 2013;31:2895),
    • peme + Bev ↑ PFS (AVAPERL JCO 2013;31:3004),
    • GEM ↑ PFS (JCO 2012;30:3516)

    # Subsequent Therapies:

    Checkpoint inhibitors encouraged:

    • nivolumab (NEJM 2015;373:1627),
    • pembrolizumab (Lancet 2016;387:1540),
    • atezolizumab (Lancet 2016;387:1837),
    • ramucirumab + docetaxel (Lancet 2014;384:665),

    # Limited Metastatic Sites: (oligo-metastases):

    Consider surgery or radiation

    # RT to Symptomatic Sites:

    lung, brain (WBRT or SRS), bone lesions, SVC syndrome

    # Bone Metastases:

    Denosumab or zoledronic acid reduces skeletal events

    # Targeted Therapy

    # EGFR-mutant: TKI.

    • 第一代erlotinib, gefitinib,
    • 第二代afatinib,

    ↑ PFS, improved RR/PFS compared to plat doublet in 1st-line, but not OS (IPASS NEJM 2010;362:2380).

    # 副作用:

    • Rash (30–50% pts, Tx: Moisturizers, abx, topical steroids)
    • diarrhea

    # Subsequent Tx:

    • osimertinib if T790M+ (50% resistance cases) (NEJM 2015;372:1689) or progressive CNS dz,
    • chemotherapy if T790M- or continuation of TKI (erlotinib, gefitinib, afatinib) ± local therapy for progression (JCO 2013;31:3335).

    # ALK-rearranged:

    # Subsequent Tx:

    • rebiopsy & choose agent ceRitinib or criZotinib based on sensitivity of resistance Mt (Cancer Discov 2016;6:1118).
    • Pemetrexed is preferred chemotherapy.

    # ROS1-rearranged:

    • Crizotinib (RR~72%) (NEJM 2014;371:1963).
    • Toxicity: Fluid retention, visual changes.
    • Resistant dz is not sensitive to alectinib or ceritinib.

    # MET (amplification or Exon 14 skipping Mt):

    • criZotinib

    # RET-rearranged:

    • caboZantinib, venDatinib

    # BRAF (V600E):

    • daBrafenib + traMetinib or vemuRafenib + cobiMetinib

    # HER2/ERBB2:

    • Trastuzumab, aFatinib

    # KRAS:

    • No targeted therapy available. May indicate higher RR w/ anti-PD-L1.
