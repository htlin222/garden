    # Basic Workup

    # Suspected or proven metastatic adenocarcinoma

    • Colonoscopy
    • Chest/abdominal/pelvic CT
    • CBC, chemistry profile, CEA
    • Determination of tumor gene status
      • for RAS and BRAF mutations and HER2 amplifications
      • individually or as part of tissue- or blood-based next-generation sequencing
    • Determination of tumor MMR or MSI statuse (if not previously done)
    • Biopsy, if clinically indicated

    # Consider PET-CT scan (skull base to mid-thigh)

    • if potentially surgically curable M1 disease in selected cases

    # Consider MRI of liver for liver metastases that are potentially resectable

    • If potentially resectable, then multidisciplinary team evaluation, including a surgeon experienced in the resection of hepatobiliary or lung metastases

    # Pathophysciology of metastatic colon cancer

    :::columns

    :::split

    • BRAF mutations are a poor prognostic factor in metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC)
    • immune checkpoint inhibitors offer a significant survival benefit to mCRC with both BRAF mutations and microsatellite instability (MSI)

    # Bevacizumab in MCRC First-Line Treatment
    • bevacizumab to oxaliplatin-based chemotherapy improved PFS in first-line trial in patients with MCRC
    • Overall survival differences did not reach statistical significance
    • Response rate was not improved by the addition of bevacizumab

    # TRIBE, NEJM 2014;371:1609

    Loupakis F, Cremolini C, Masi G, et al. Initial Therapy with FOLFOXIRI and Bevacizumab for Metastatic Colorectal Cancer. New England Journal of Medicine. 2014;371(17):1609-1618. doi:10.1056/NEJMoa1403108

    :::columns

    • A phase 2 study
    • 508 patients with untreated metastatic colorectal cancer
    • randomly assigned to receive either
      • FOLFIRI plus bevacizumab (control group)
      • FOLFOXIRI plus bevacizumab (experimental group)
    • Median progression-free survival was 12.1 months in the experimental group compared to 9.7 months in the control group
    • FOLFOXIRI plus bevacizumab improved outcomes

    :::split

    # EGFR Signal Transduction Pathway
    • EGFR is a receptor tyrosine kinase that belongs to the ERBB family
    • Ligand binding to EGFR causes receptor dimerization
    • The resulting receptor autophosphorylation initiates signal transduction
    • Cetuximab inhibits receptor phosphorylation

    :::

    • Pembrolizumab is superior to chemotherapy with respect to progression-free survival

    # Principles of Managing Patients With Metastatic Colorectal Cancer

    # Initial evaluation and choice of treatment intent and modality

    • Poor functional status, including life expectancy <6 mo
      • palliative care
      • Manage bowel obstruction or bleeding with stent or palliative radiation
    • Symptomatic bowel obstruction or significant bleeding
      • Surgical management with diverting ostomy
    • Significant comorbidity, frailty, or both but life expectancy ‚â•6 mo
      • Single-agent fluorouracil or capecitabine palliates symptoms and extends life expectancy
      • Systemic therapy can be intensified or discontinued based on tolerance

    # Metastatic disease that is unresectable and previously resected or adequately controlled primary tumor (unresectable metastases)

    • systemic chemotherapy (such as FOLFOX or FOLFIRI) or immunotherapy in MSI-H/MMR-D
    • Obtain molecular tumor profiling to inform treatment selection
    • typical to survive 2 to 3 years with intensive treatment

    # Rectal primary tumor

    • Include radiation oncology
    • Radiation is rarely required as initial therapy when metastases are present
      • may induce myelosuppression which impairs future tolerance of systemic treatment

    # Right-sided primary tumor

    • Avoid unnecessary resection of the primary tumor; obstruction is rare
    • Avoid use of first-line cetuximab and panitumumab given low response rates
    • Right-sided colon cancer (RC) is distinct from left-sided colorectal cancer (LC)
      • in embryology, pathology, and genetics
      • RC has more KRAS and BRAF mutations and
      • RC has poorer clinical outcomes than LC
    • Shorter OS in multivariate analysis
      • BRAF mutations
      • RC
      • poorly differentiated histology
      • and peritoneal involvement were associated with

    JCO 2016;34(suppl, abstr 3504, 3505, 3506; JAMA Oncol 2017;3:211)

    # Prior adjuvant chemotherapy

    • If metastases develop within 6 months of adjuvant single-agent fluorouracil, intensification to multiagent regimens such as FOLFOX or FOLFIRI is necessary
    • If metastases develop within 6 months of adjuvant FOLFOX, use FOLFIRI

    # Anticipated need for surgery

    • Avoid VEGF inhibitors for 6 to 8 weeks before and after major surgery

    # Significant peripheral neuropathy from comorbid illness such as diabetes or adjuvant oxaliplatin

    • Avoid or delay use of oxaliplatin to prevent worsening peripheral neuropathy
    • Vigilance with close monitoring is required with dose reduction or discontinuation before neuropathy limits function

    # Hyperbilirubinemia

    • Avoid or delay use of irinotecan, which is metabolized in the liver

    # Fluorouracil-associated coronary vasospasm

    • Comanage with cardiology. Infusional fluorouracil is more likely to lead to vasospasm and should be avoided. Consider vasodilators and inpatient management

    # Severe pancytopenia; mucositis after fluorouracil treatment

    • Evaluate for dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase deficiency and reduce the dose based on results. Consider IROX (irinotecan and oxaliplatin) regimen in cases of homozygous dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase deficiency

    # For FOLFOX, OX should be discontinued after first 3 months of Rx till progression

    • Max 12 dose, To reduce risk of severe neurotoxicity
    • Can be resumed at or after progression (OPTIMOX1, JCO 2006;24:394)

    Tournigand C, Cervantes A, Figer A, et al. OPTIMOX1: a randomized study of FOLFOX4 or FOLFOX7 with oxaliplatin in a stop-and-Go fashion in advanced colorectal cancer–a GERCOR study. J Clin Oncol. 2006;24(3):394-400. doi:10.1200/JCO.2005.03.0106

    # Adverse Reactions >10% of oxaliplatin

    • Gastrointestinal: Abdominal pain (31%), anorexia (20%), constipation (31%), diarrhea (46%; grades 3/4: 4%), nausea (64%; grades 3/4: 4%), stomatitis (2% to 14%), vomiting (37%; grades 3/4: 4%)
    • Hematologic & oncologic: Anemia (64%; grades 3/4: 1%), leukopenia (13%). thrombocytopenia (30%; grades 3/4: 3%)
    • Hepatic: Increased serum alanine aminotransferase (36%), increased serum alkaline phosphatase (42%), increased serum aspartate aminotransferase (54%), increased serum bilirubin (13%)
    • Nervous system: Fatigue (61%), headache (13%), insomnia (11%), pain (14%), peripheral neuropathy (76%, grades 3/4: 7%; acute: 65%, grades 3/4: 5%; delayed [persistent]: 43%, grades 3/4: 3%)
    • Neuromuscular & skeletal: Back pain (11%)
    • Respiratory: Cough (11%), dyspnea (13%)
    • Miscellaneous: Fever (25%)

    # Severe diarrhea following irinotecan containing regimens

    • Reduce dose based on symptoms and intensify antidiarrheal medications
    • Consider testing for UGT1A1 polymorphisms and reduce dose based on results

    Schulz C, Heinemann V, Schalhorn A, et al. UGT1A1 gene polymorphism: Impact on toxicity and efficacy of irinotecan-based regimens in metastatic colorectal cancer. World J Gastroenterol. 2009;15(40):5058-5066. doi:10.3748/wjg.15.5058

    # Frailty after second-line treatment (third line if KRAS/NRAS wild type)

    • Consider palliative and supportive care over use of later-line systemic treatments with marginal survival advantage and substantial toxicity

    # Somatic variant in the KRAS or NRAS genes

    • Avoid use of cetuximab or panitumumab as these agents are not effective for patients with these molecular features

    # Somatic variant in the BRAF gene

    • Recognize poor prognosis, prioritize clinical trials
    • Use second-line therapy with encorafenib plus cetuximab

    # Somatic variant in the ERBB2 gene

    • Drugs that block the ERBB2 receptor like trastuzumab may work but prioritize clinical trials over their off-label use

    # Microsatellite instability-high

    • Prioritize immunotherapy containing regimens

    # Take Home message Part I

    • Cetuximab and panitumumab extend median survival by 2-4 months for KRAS/NRAS/BRAF wild-type tumors
    • Effective targeted therapies are not yet available for KRAS or NRAS sequence variations
    • Combination therapy with BRAF and EGFR inhibitors extended overall survival to 9.3 months
      • for BRAF V600E sequence variations
    • Immunotherapy may be used for microsatellite instability or mismatch repair deficiency
    • Median overall survival of 31.4 months in previously treated patients with immunotherapy
