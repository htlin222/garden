# PUD 🚧 


>  A peptic ulcer is a sore that forms in the lining of your digestive tract. Peptic ulcers can occur in the stomach (gastric ulcer), esophagus (esophageal ulcer), or the first part of the small intestine (duodenal ulcer). Ulcers are usually caused by an infection with the Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) bacteria, less often by the use of certain medications, or, in a small number of cases, by stress or other psychological factors
