# Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria

(PNH) (Blood 2009;113:6522)

* Acquired clonal stem cell disorder = inactivating somatic mutation of PIG-A gene → deficiency of GPI-anchor for CD55 & CD59 (inhib of complement) → complement-mediated RBC lysis, plt aggregation, & hypercoagulability

* Clinical: intravascular hemolytic anemia, hypercoagulability (venous >arterial; esp. intraabdominal, cerebral), smooth muscle dystonias, deficient hematopoiesis (cytopenias); a/w aplastic anemia, MDS and evolution to AML

* Dx: flow cytometry (↓ CD55 & CD59) on RBCs and granulocytes; urine hemosiderosis

* [[Treatment-Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria]]


## Explain by ChatGPT

PNH is an acquired **clonal stem cell** disorder caused by an inactivating somatic mutation of the **PIG-A gene**. This mutation leads to a deficiency of **GPI-anchor for CD55 and CD59**, which are inhibitors of complement. This deficiency results in complement-mediated RBC lysis, plt aggregation, and hypercoagulability.

The clinical manifestations of PNH are intravascular hemolytic anemia, hypercoagulability, and venous thrombosis. The diagnosis of PNH is made by flow cytometry, which shows a decrease in CD55 and CD59 on RBCs and granulocytes. Urine hemosiderosis is also seen in patients with PNH.

 Treatment for PNH includes **anticoagulation, RBC transfusion, and splenectomy**.
