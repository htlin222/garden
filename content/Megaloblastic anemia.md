# Megaloblastic anemia


* Impaired DNA synthesis → cytoplasm matures faster than nucleus → ineffective erythropoiesis and macrocytosis; due to folate or B12 deficiency; also in MDS

* ✓ folate and vitamin B12; ↑ LDH & indirect bilirubin (due to ineffective erythropoiesis)

* Smear: neutrophil hypersegmentation, macro-ovalocytes, anisocytosis, poikilocytosis



## Explain by ChatGPT

1. Megaloblastic anemia is a condition in which the body's red blood cells do not mature properly.

2. This can be due to a deficiency of folate or vitamin B12, which are essential for DNA synthesis.

3. Megaloblastic anemia can also be caused by myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS), a condition in which the bone marrow does not produce enough healthy blood cells.

4. Symptoms of megaloblastic anemia include fatigue, shortness of breath, and pale skin.

5. A blood smear may show evidence of neutrophil hypersegmentation, macro-ovalocytes, anisocytosis, and poikilocytosis.
