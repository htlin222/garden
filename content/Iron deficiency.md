# Iron deficiency

(Lancet 2021;397:233)

* ↓ marrow iron & depleted body iron stores → ↓ heme synthesis → microcytosis → anemia

* Special clinical manifestations: angular cheilosis, atrophic glossitis, pica (consumption of nonnutritive substances such as ice, clay), koilonychia (nail spooning)

Plummer-Vinson syndrome (iron deficiency anemia, esophageal web & atrophic glossitis)

* Etiologies: chronic bleeding (GI—incl. cancer, menstrual, parasites, NSAIDs, etc.), ↓ supply (malnutrition; ↓ absorp. due to celiac sprue, Crohn’s, ↑ gastric pH, subtotal gastrectomy), ↑ demand (preg; Blood 2017;129:940). Iron-refractory iron-defic. anemia (IRIDA; rare genetic disorder due to hepcidin dysregulation; Nat Genet 2008;40:569).

* Diagnosis (eval ideally before Rx): ↓ Fe, ↑ TIBC, ↓ ferritin (esp. <15), ↓ transferrin sat (Fe/TIBC; esp. <15%), ↑ soluble transferrin receptor; ↑ plt. Unless hx c/w other etiology, initiate workup for GIB, incl. H. pylori serology. ? Celiac labs (anti-TTG, anti-endomysial IgA Abs). Cytogenetics & molecular testing as indicated (eg, MDS, leukemia).

* Treatment: oral Fe TID (~6 wks to correct anemia; ~6 mo to replete Fe stores; nb, oral Fe does not give ⊕ Hemoccult). With severe anemia, persistent losses, prior to Epo Rx, or while inpatient, use IV iron (Fe-sucrose, -gluconate, -dextran).

* Iron levels in the marrow are decreased and the body's iron stores are depleted, which leads to decreased heme synthesis and microcytosis (smaller than normal red blood cells). This can cause anemia.

* Special clinical manifestations: angular cheilosis (cracks in the corners of the mouth), atrophic glossitis (inflammation of the tongue), pica (consumption of nonnutritive substances such as ice, clay), koilonychia (nail spooning)

Plummer-Vinson syndrome (iron deficiency anemia, esophageal web & atrophic glossitis)

* Etiologies: chronic bleeding (from the GI tract, including cancer, menstrual bleeding, parasites, NSAIDs, etc.), decreased supply (malnutrition, decreased absorption due to celiac sprue, Crohn's disease, increased gastric pH, subtotal gastrectomy), increased demand (pregnancy; Blood 2017;129:940). Iron-refractory iron-deficiency anemia (IRIDA; rare genetic disorder due to hepcidin dysregulation; Nat Genet 2008;40:569).

* Diagnosis (evaluation ideally done before starting treatment): decreased iron levels, increased total iron-binding capacity, decreased ferritin (especially <15), decreased transferrin saturation (Fe/TIBC; especially <15%), increased soluble transferrin receptor; increased platelet count. Unless the history is consistent with another etiology, initiate workup for gastrointestinal bleeding, including H. pylori serology. Consider celiac disease labs (anti-TTG, anti-endomysial IgA antibodies). Cytogenetics and molecular testing as indicated (eg, myelodysplastic syndrome, leukemia).

* Treatment: oral iron three times daily (~6 wks to correct anemia; ~6 mo to replete iron stores; note, oral iron does not give a positive Hemoccult test). With severe anemia, persistent losses, prior to erythropoietin treatment, or while inpatient, use IV iron (Fe-sucrose, -gluconate, -dextran).

＃ 缺鐵

（柳葉刀 2021；397:233）

* 骨髓中的鐵水平下降，身體的鐵儲備耗盡，導致血紅素合成減少和小紅細胞增多（小於正常紅細胞）。這會導致貧血。

* 特殊臨床表現：口角乾裂（嘴角開裂）、萎縮性舌炎（舌頭髮炎）、異食癖（消耗非營養物質，如冰、粘土）、反甲（指甲勺）

Plummer-Vinson 綜合徵（缺鐵性貧血、食管蹼和萎縮性舌炎）

* 病因：慢性出血（來自胃腸道，包括癌症、月經出血、寄生蟲、非甾體抗炎藥等）、供應減少（營養不良、腹腔口炎引起的吸收減少、克羅恩病、胃 pH 值升高、胃大部切除術）、需求增加（懷孕；Blood 2017；129:940）。鐵難治性缺鐵性貧血（IRIDA；鐵調素失調引起的罕見遺傳病；Nat Genet 2008；40：569）。

* 診斷（最好在開始治療前進行評估）：鐵水平降低，總鐵結合能力增加，鐵蛋白降低（尤其是 <15），轉鐵蛋白飽和度降低（Fe/TIBC；尤其是 <15%），可溶性轉鐵蛋白受體增加；血小板計數增加。除非病史與另一種病因一致，否則開始檢查胃腸道出血，包括幽門螺桿菌血清學。考慮乳糜瀉實驗室（抗 TTG、抗肌內膜 IgA 抗體）。指定的細胞遺傳學和分子檢測（例如，骨髓增生異常綜合徵、白血病）。

* 治療：每日 3 次口服鐵劑（約 6 週糾正貧血；約 6 個月補充鐵儲備；注意，口服鐵劑不會產生陽性 Hemoccult 試驗）。對於嚴重貧血、持續損失，在促紅細胞生成素治療之前或在住院期間，使用靜脈鐵劑（Fe-蔗糖、-葡萄糖酸鹽、-葡聚醣）。
