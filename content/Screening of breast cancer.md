# Screening of breast cancer
 (JAMA 2015;314:1599; Annals 2019;170:547)
 
* Mammography: ~20–30% ↓ in breast cancer mortality, smaller abs. benefit in women <50 y (JAMA 2018;319:1814); digital breast tomosynthesis (3-D) ↑ specificity (JAMA Oncol 2019;5:635); suspicious findings: clustered microcalcifications, spiculated, enlarging
* ACS: annual mammo starting at 45 (consider biennial after 54), cont. if life expect ≥10 y
[[癌症篩檢介紹]]
* USPSTF: screen biennially ages 50–75 (some may want to begin at 40)
* ↑ risk: screen earlier w/ exam and mammo (age 25 in BRCA1/2 carrier, 5–10 y before earliest FHx case, 8–10 y after thoracic RT, upon dx of ↑ risk benign disease)
* MRI: superior to mammo in high-risk and young Pts; consider annually if >20% lifetime risk (eg, ⊕ FHx, BRCA1/2, prior chest RT) (Lancet 2011;378:1804)
* Germline genetic testing: if metastatic, TNBC, ♂, age ≤45y, or by FHx (NCCN v1.2022)


## Explain by ChatGPT

1. Mammography: A mammogram is an x-ray of the breast. It is the best screening test for breast cancer, and can find cancers when they are too small to feel. Mammograms can also find microcalcifications, which are tiny deposits of calcium in the breast tissue. Microcalcifications are not usually cancer, but they can be a sign of cancer.

2. ACS: The American Cancer Society recommends that women at average risk of breast cancer start mammograms at **age 45**. Women at high risk of breast cancer may need to start mammograms earlier.

3. USPSTF: The United States Preventive Services Task Force recommends that women ages 50 to 75 get a mammogram every two years. Some women may want to start mammograms at age 40.

4. ↑ risk: Women at increased risk for breast cancer should talk to their doctor about when to start mammograms. This includes women with a family history of breast cancer, women who have had radiation therapy to the chest, and women with certain benign breast conditions.

5. MRI: Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is a type of scan that can find breast cancers that are too small to be seen on a mammogram. MRI is better than mammography for screening high-risk women and young women. MRI may be recommended annually for women at high risk for breast cancer.

6. Germline genetic testing: Germline genetic testing is a test to find out if you have inherited a gene mutation that increases your risk for certain types of cancer. This test is recommended for people with metastatic breast cancer, triple-negative breast cancer, male breast cancer, or breast cancer at age 45 or younger. It is also recommended for people with a family history of breast cancer.
