# introduction of Budd-Chiari syndrome

* Budd-Chiari syndrome is a rare vascular disorder caused by obstruction of hepatic blood outflow at the level of the hepatic veins or hepatic portion of the inferior vena cava.
* The symptoms of classical BCS were first described in the 1800s by Budd and later by Chiari.
* Diagnostic and therapeutic techniques have evolved, making it easier to identify the precise location of the obstruction.
* The location of the obstruction is clinically and prognostically significant.
* The lexicon of Budd-Chiari syndrome has evolved over time to include many eponyms, which reflect the heterogeneous presentation of the syndrome and the possibility of distinct entities within it.
