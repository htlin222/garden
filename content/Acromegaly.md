---
title: Acromegaly
---


(↑ GH; 10% of adenomas; Nat Rev Dis Primer 2019;5:1)
* Physiology:
	* stimulates secretion of insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1)
* Clinical manifestations:
	* ↑ soft tissue, arthralgias, jaw enlargement, headache, carpal tunnel syndrome, macroglossia, hoarseness, sleep apnea, amenorrhea, impotence, diabetes mellitus, acanthosis/skin tags, ↑ sweating, HTN/CMP, colonic polyps
* Diagnostic studies:
	* low utility in checking random GH levels because of pulsatile secretion
	* ↑ IGF-1 (somatomedin C); ± ↑ PRL; OGTT → GH not suppressed to <1 (<0.3 if newer assay) ng/mL; pituitary MRI to evaluate for tumor
* Treatment:
	* surgery, **octreotide** (long- and short-acting preparations), dopamine agonists (if PRL cosecretion), pegvisomant (GH receptor antagonist), radiation
* Prognosis: 
	* w/ and w/o Rx ↑ mortality, risk of pituitary insufficiency, colon cancer
