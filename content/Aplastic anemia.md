# Aplastic anemia

 (stem cell failure) (NEJM 2015;373:35)

* Epidemiology: 2–5 cases/106/y; biphasic (major peak in adolescents, 2nd peak in elderly)

* Diagnosis: pancytopenia w/ ↓ retics, BM bx w/ hypocellularity, usually nl cytogenetics

## Explain by ChatGPT

Aplastic anemia is a stem cell failure that occurs 2-5 cases per 106 people per year. It is biphasic, meaning that it has a major peak in adolescents and a second peak in the elderly. The diagnosis is pancytopenia, or a decrease in the number of red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets, with a bone marrow biopsy showing hypocellularity, or a decrease in the number of cells. The etiology, or cause, of aplastic anemia is unknown in most cases, but it can be caused by certain drugs, chemicals, or infections. The treatment and prognosis, or outlook, of aplastic anemia depend on the severity of the condition.
